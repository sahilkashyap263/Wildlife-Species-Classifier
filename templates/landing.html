Three Ways to Scan

Choose the modality that fits your field conditions — or combine them all for maximum accuracy.

Audio Mode

Upload or record a 15-second acoustic sample. Our CNN processes mel-spectrograms across overlapping chunks to identify species from calls, songs, and ambient sound.

  • WAV, MP3, M4A & WebM support
  • Live 15s microphone recording
  • GBR distance estimation (Near / Medium / Far)

Fusion Mode

Feed both audio and image simultaneously. The fusion engine weighs both predictions, checks inter-modal agreement, and applies a confidence penalty on conflict.

  • Weighted multi-modal fusion (58% image / 42% audio)
  • Agreement detection & conflict penalty
  • Combined distance + visual metadata
Sensor Fusion

Visual Mode

Upload a photo or capture from your camera. The image CNN classifies species and extracts frame coverage, habitat zone, activity level, and size class.

  • JPG, PNG, WebP support
  • Live camera with corner-finder overlay
  • Visual metadata: habitat, activity, size

Pipeline

How It Works

From raw sensor input to structured species identification in under a second.

01

Upload Input

Provide an audio file, image, or both. Record live from your microphone or capture from your webcam directly in the browser.

02

CNN Inference

Audio is chunked into overlapping 4-second mel-spectrograms. Images are resized to 224×224. Both pass through trained CNNs.

03

Fusion & Distance

The GBR distance engine estimates range from acoustic features. In fusion mode, results are weighted and agreement is checked.

04

Structured Output

Species, confidence, distance, habitat, diet, conservation status and behaviour — all logged to your personal history.
